The founding

The law was never the problem. The translation was.

“I kept watching people sign things they didn't understand, forfeit rights they didn't know they had.”

Cause & Counsel began as a simple frustration. Legislation that governs ordinary life — tenancy, employment, consumer protection, fundamental rights — is written for those already fluent in it. Everyone else is left to guess.

Founded by Sanaya Parikh, a third-year student at Jindal Global Law School, the initiative branched from We Kids Care, her high-school project on civic awareness. The conviction carried over: knowledge of your rights should never depend on who you can afford to ask.

Today we are a peer-reviewed, India-focused collective writing the explainers we wish existed — rigorous enough for the classroom, clear enough for the kitchen table.

Sanaya Parikh — Founder, JGLS '27

Write with us

Submit an article.

We publish clear, rigorous writing from law students across India. Every piece is peer-reviewed. Here's what we look for.

  • Plain English first. If a sixteen-year-old can't follow it, rewrite it.
  • One idea, done well. A single statute, case, or right — explained completely.
  • Cite your sources. Link the bare act, the judgment, the section.
  • Original work only. No AI-generated drafts, no recycled coursework.
  • India-focused. Grounded in Indian law and the lives it touches.
  • 800–2,000 words. Long enough to be useful, short enough to be read.
