Who we are

Built on a simple conviction.

That knowing your rights should never depend on whether you can afford a lawyer to explain them. Everything we make starts there.

The founder

SP

Sanaya Parikh · Founder

“The law was never the problem. The translation was — and translation is something a student can do.”

Sanaya Parikh is a third-year student at Jindal Global Law School, O.P. Jindal Global University, New Delhi. She founded Cause & Counsel in 2025 to do one thing well: take the law that governs ordinary life and write it back in language ordinary people can use.

The instinct isn't new for her. In high school she started We Kids Care, a civic-awareness initiative that taught younger students about their everyday rights. Cause & Counsel is that idea grown up — rigorous, peer-reviewed, and built for an audience that extends well beyond the classroom.

She leads a small collective of law students who believe the same thing she does: that access to legal knowledge is a quiet but enormous form of justice, and that you don't need a bar licence to widen it.

✦ JGLS '27 ✦ Founder & Editor ✦ We Kids Care alumna

What we do

Four pillars, one purpose.

✦ 01

Research & Writing

Peer-reviewed explainers and case analyses that break legislation down to first principles. We cite the bare act, link the judgment, and refuse to hide behind jargon. The goal is always the same: a reader finishes the piece knowing exactly where they stand.

✦ 02

Rights Education

Plain-English guides to the rights people actually use — as tenants, employees, consumers, and citizens. Less theory, more "here is what you can do on Monday morning." Knowing a right exists is the first step to exercising it.

✦ 03

Community Outreach

Workshops that take legal literacy out of the lecture hall and into schools, colleges, and community spaces. We partner with organisations already doing the work and give them a clear, accurate legal layer to stand on.

✦ 04

Startup Support

Founder-friendly primers on incorporation, contracts, intellectual property, and compliance — the law that quietly shapes a young company. Written for the founder who can't yet afford counsel but can't afford to get it wrong either.

Our mission

To make the law a tool for everyone — not a privilege for the few who can read it.
